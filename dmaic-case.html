DMAIC Framework Case

Systematic Problem-Solving for Urine Creatinine Measurement Deviation in Laboratory Quality Control.

1. DEFINE

Situation & Task

Significant and unreasonable deviations were observed between standard samples and actual measurements, undermining data credibility.

Goal

  • Identify root cause of deviation.
  • Restore results to acceptable range.
  • Ensure 100% accurate & reproducible data.

2. MEASURE

Actions Taken

  • Audited preparation/storage records.
  • Rule out contamination via control checks.
  • Monitored lab ambient temperature and equilibration times.

Data Collected

Quantitative analysis revealed systematic high deviation correlated with specific shifts and environmental fluctuations.

3. ANALYZE

Methodology

Applied Fishbone Diagram and "5 Whys" to examine Reagents, Handling, Environment, and Instrumentation.

Root Cause

Inconsistent lab temperature caused thermal differences between standards and samples, directly impacting reaction kinetics.

Impact: Temperature variation skewed UV-Vis absorption peaks by altering reactant solubility and kinetics.

4. IMPROVE

Action Plan

  • Revised SOP for mandatory 30-min temperature equilibration.
  • Stabilized reagent incubation procedures.
  • Conducted cross-training for lab technicians.

Improvement Result

Measurement values returned to normal range with CV < 3%. Reliable data restored for all patient records.

Result: Restored consistent measurement performance and eliminated outlier data.

5. CONTROL

Sustainability

  • Formalized SOP as standard protocol.
  • Integrated temperature logging into Daily QC.
  • Established periodic audit of control limit charts.

Outcome

Reduced review time and maintained zero systematic deviation for over 12 consecutive months.
