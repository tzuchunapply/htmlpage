DMAIC Case Study: Urine Creatinine Measurement Deviation

Quality Control Optimization in Clinical Analytical Chemistry
Figure 1: Investigation & Resolution Workflow for Control Sample Deviation
Phase 1

DEFINE

Situation & Task

During routine urine creatinine analysis, significant and unreasonable deviations were observed between standard samples and actual measurements, undermining overall data credibility.

Goal
  • Identify the root cause of the deviation.
  • Restore measurement results to an acceptable range.
  • Ensure accurate and reproducible data for patient results.
Phase 2

MEASURE

Actions
  • Reviewed and compared experimental records to detect deviations in operating conditions.
  • Audited preparation and storage records of standards to rule out contamination.
  • Monitored environmental factors, specifically lab temperature and sample equilibration times.
Measurements

Collected quantitative data showing systematic deviations under varying temperature conditions (High standard deviation in QC checks).

Phase 3

ANALYZE

Root Cause Analysis Tools

Applied Fishbone Diagram and "5 Whys" to assess reagent preparation, handling, and environmental effects.

Analysis Result: Identified that inconsistent laboratory temperature caused significant thermal differences between standards and samples, affecting reaction kinetics and UV-Vis measurement accuracy.
Phase 4

IMPROVE

Implementation
  • Revised SOPs to ensure mandatory temperature equilibration of all standards and samples before UV-Vis analysis.
  • Implemented a "stabilization period" for reagents after removal from storage.
Results

Measurement values returned to normal range with high reproducibility and reliability.

Phase 5

CONTROL

Standardization
  • Formalized the improved SOP as the routine protocol for all lab personnel.
  • Established a periodic monitoring log for lab ambient temperature.
Outcome

Achieved reduced review time and reliable results. No further systematic deviations recorded since implementation.
