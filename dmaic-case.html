DMAIC Case Study

Systematic investigation of urine creatinine measurement deviation caused by sample temperature mismatch.

1. DEFINE

Situation & Task

During urine creatinine analysis, significant and unreasonable deviations were observed in standard samples (Control Sample OOC), undermining overall data credibility.

Goals

  • Identify the root cause of the deviation.
  • Restore measurement results to an acceptable range.
  • Ensure experimental legitimacy and compliance.

2. MEASURE

Monitoring Actions

  • Reviewed experimental records for procedural deviations.
  • Checked reagent preparation to rule out contamination.
  • Monitored lab temperature, humidity, and refrigerator temperature.

Measurements

Reviewed collected data to identify if systematic deviations occurred simultaneously with the control sample OOC events.

3. ANALYZE

Root Cause Methodology

Assessed factors: reagent prep, handling, and environment. Conducted root cause analysis using the "5 Whys" technique.

Technical Analysis Result

UV-Vis results showed deviations due to inconsistent reaction kinetics between creatinine and Picrate.

Analysis: Reaction speed is temperature-sensitive. Patient samples were at room temperature, while control samples were taken directly from the refrigerator. This temperature mismatch invalidated the control as a standard.

4. IMPROVE

Actions Implemented

  • Updated SOP to ensure mandatory temperature equilibration of control samples before analysis.
  • Trained lab personnel to strictly follow revised equilibration procedures.

Improvement Result

Measurement values successfully returned to the normal/expected range, ensuring consistent analytical performance.

Result: Eliminated the thermal kinetic gap between patient and control samples.

5. CONTROL

Standardization

  • Formalized the improved SOP as the routine protocol for all future experiments.
  • Established periodic monitoring of lab temperature and measurement consistency logs.

Outcome

Maintained accurate and reproducible measurements. Provided a reliable operational reference for ongoing and future clinical analyses.
