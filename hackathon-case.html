Phase 2: YOLOv5 & Preprocessing

Enhancing Data Quality

To improve detection of subtle atomic defects (Vacancy & Replacement), we applied professional image filters:

  • Gaussian & Median Filters: Reduced background noise from microscopy.
  • Contrast Adjustment: Increased clarity (~8.71 ratio) for atomic lattice observation.
  • "Ice" Color Map: Provided optimal visualization for defect contrast.

Figure 1: Comparison between raw microscopy data and preprocessed images using Gaussian filters and color mapping.

Technical Verification: Intensity Profiles

Figure 2: Intensity line profiles used to verify model predictions against physical atomic intensity drops.

Cross-Referencing Model Predictions

To ensure model validity, we utilized Intensity Line Profiles to analyze the atomic lattice:

  • Broken Regions: Confirmed by near-zero intensity peaks in the profile.
  • Atomic Vacancies: Identified by specific periodic dips in intensity, matching theoretical Mo/S vacancy behaviors.
  • High Precision: This physical verification confirmed the model's high reliability in defect classification.

Model Generalizability

The trained YOLOv5 model was tested on MoS₂ images sourced from Academic Literature (ACS Nano, Nano Letters). It successfully identified defects across different image sources and instrument settings, demonstrating robust generalizability for semiconductor process inspection.
